AIDEN is the enterprise-specific reasoning layer that sits on top of commodity governance shells. It helps engineering teams author strong proposals and gives human reviewers structured evidence packs instead of slide decks.

Demo supports three modes: Deterministic (scripted, no API), Shadow (side-by-side comparison), and Live Assist (real agent calls). Select mode in each agent tab.

Architect Agent

Guides teams through building proposals that will pass review. Catches gaps in real time, suggests remediations, generates architecture views before any reviewer sees the work.

Reviewer Agent

Four-outcome evaluator (pass / fail / abstain / degrade) runs against pinned policies. Produces R_eff scores, evidence packs, and explicit gate decisions with human oversight.

Delta Engine

Computes the minimum set of changes to reach APPROVE. Ranks items by R_eff impact per unit effort. Searches exception history for viable waiver paths.

Exception Intelligence

Learns from prior approvals, waivers, and rejections. Surfaces how similar situations were resolved, with estimated approval probability and suggested conditions.

What AIDEN Owns (The Brain)

Enterprise-specific reasoning no vendor can ship:

  • Policy interpretation — Your tiers, waiver semantics, tier-rule mappings.
  • Exception history — Which exception paths have been approved, by whom, with what rationale.
  • Waiver semantics — What constitutes a valid waiver, who can grant it, how long it lasts.
  • Authoring logic — Minimum delta to move a proposal from Draft to ReviewReady.
  • SoD paths — Separation-of-duties rules tied to your actual org structure.

What We Integrate (The Shell)

Commodity products that handle surfaces and generic governance:

  • ServiceNow — Workflow routing, ticket management, approval chains.
  • LeanIX — Architecture catalog, dependency mapping, portfolio views.
  • Backstage — Developer portal, service catalog, tech radar.
  • Credo AI / watsonx — Generic AI scorecards, policy-pack scaffolding.

Guard → Gate Separation

Guards and gates are deliberately separate concerns:

  • Guard — What the evidence says. Four outcomes: pass, fail, abstain, degrade.
  • Gate — What to do about it. Thresholds, SoD checks, overrides, waivers.
  • Fold behavior — Engine errors fold to BLOCK with human escalation.
  • Decision log — Every gate decision records outcome, rationale, tier, waivers applied.

R_eff Scoring

Effective reliability computed per section, overall = weakest link:

  • R_raw — passed / (passed + failed + degraded). Abstained rules excluded.
  • Penalty — critical_fails × 0.05 + high_fails × 0.03.
  • Degradation — degraded_count × 0.02.
  • R_eff — max(0, R_raw − penalty − degradation). Display: round(R_eff × 100)
  • Overall — min(R_eff_i) across all sections. Weakest link, not average.

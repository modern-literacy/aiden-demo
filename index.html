AIDEN is the enterprise-specific reasoning layer that sits on top of commodity governance shells. It helps engineering teams author strong proposals and gives human reviewers structured evidence packs instead of slide decks.

Demo runs on seeded synthetic data — realistic precedents and reviewer behavior. Real org memory accumulates over time.

Author Copilot

Guides teams through building proposals that will pass review. Catches gaps in real time, suggests remediations, generates architecture views before any reviewer sees the work.

Review Engine

Four-outcome evaluator (pass / fail / abstain / degrade) runs against pinned policies. Produces R_eff scores, evidence packs, and explicit gate decisions with human oversight.

Delta Engine

Computes the minimum set of changes to reach APPROVE. Ranks items by R_eff impact per unit effort. Searches exception history for viable waiver paths.

Exception Intelligence

Learns from prior approvals, waivers, and rejections. Surfaces how similar situations were resolved, with estimated approval probability and suggested conditions.

What AIDEN Owns (The Brain)

Enterprise-specific reasoning no vendor can ship:

  • Policy interpretation — Your tiers, waiver semantics, tier-rule mappings. Reflects decades of compliance decisions and audit findings.
  • Exception history — Which exception paths have been approved, by whom, with what rationale and precedent weight.
  • Waiver semantics — What constitutes a valid waiver, who can grant it, how long it lasts, what triggers re-evaluation.
  • Authoring logic — Minimum delta to move a proposal from Draft to ReviewReady under a specific tier and policy set.
  • SoD paths — Separation-of-duties rules tied to your actual org structure, not generic role templates.

What We Integrate (The Shell)

Commodity products that handle surfaces and generic governance:

  • ServiceNow — Workflow routing, ticket management, approval chains. UI surface for reviewers.
  • LeanIX — Architecture catalog, dependency mapping, portfolio views.
  • Backstage — Developer portal, service catalog, tech radar. Self-service surface for teams.
  • Credo AI / watsonx — Generic AI scorecards, policy-pack scaffolding. Complementary to AIDEN, not replaced by it.

Guard → Gate Separation

Guards and gates are deliberately separate concerns:

  • Guard — What the evidence says. Four outcomes: pass, fail, abstain, degrade. Deterministic. No judgment.
  • Gate — What to do about it. Thresholds, SoD checks, overrides, waivers, autonomy budgets, human judgment.
  • Fold behavior — Engine errors, timeouts, and unknowns fold to BLOCK with human escalation. Infrastructure failures are separate from proposal failures.
  • Decision log — Every gate decision records outcome, rationale, tier, waivers applied, and override requirements.

R_eff Scoring

Effective reliability computed per section, overall = weakest link:

  • R_raw — passed / (passed + failed + degraded). Abstained rules excluded from ratio.
  • Penalty — critical_fails × 0.05 + high_fails × 0.03. Severity matters, not just count.
  • Degradation — degraded_count × 0.02. Missing evidence costs confidence.
  • R_eff — max(0, R_raw − penalty − degradation). Display: round(R_eff × 100)
  • Overall — min(R_eff_i) across all sections. Weakest link, not average.
